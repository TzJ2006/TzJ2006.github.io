Breakthrough Works#

1. A low cost seasonal solar soil heat storage system for greenhouse heating: Design and pilot study (2015)#

Description: Described and tested a low-cost seasonal solar soil heat storage system for winter heating in greenhouses. This system stores solar energy in the soil during summer and utilizes it during winter, enabling thermal transfer across seasons.

Why couldn’t it be done before: Seasonal soil heat storage was difficult to implement due to its complexity and high cost. This paper proposed a simplified design and conducted field tests, reducing the engineering barriers. Without a verified low-cost prototype, the feasibility of this method was uncertain.

Impact: Became a highly cited reference in this field (125 citations), providing an economical technical approach for low-carbon heating in agricultural greenhouses. It has been widely referenced in subsequent research on energy storage and agricultural energy systems.

2. An automated optimization method for calibrating building energy simulation models with measured data: Orientation and a case study (2016)#

Description: Proposed an automated optimization method for calibrating building energy simulation models using measured data, solving the problem of discrepancies between simulation results and real-world energy consumption. This method automates the manual calibration process.

Why couldn’t it be done before: Building energy simulation models have many parameters (material thermal properties, equipment efficiency, behavior of users, etc.). Traditional manual calibration relied on engineers’ experience and was very time-consuming. Automated optimization algorithms were not yet mature in building simulation before 2016, and the computational cost limited large-scale parameter searches.

Impact: Had 87 citations, becoming an important reference for modeling methods in building energy simulation. It promoted automated calibration practices in digital twins and Building Information Modeling (BIM) fields.

3. Global sensitivity analysis of an urban microclimate system under uncertainty: Design and case study (2017)#

Description: Systematically introduced global sensitivity analysis into urban microclimate simulations, quantifying the contribution of various input parameters to the uncertainty in urban thermal environment predictions and identifying key influencing factors.

Why couldn’t it be done before: Urban microclimate model parameters were highly dimensional (building geometry, materials, vegetation, meteorology, etc.). Local sensitivity analysis couldn’t capture the interaction effects between parameters. Calculating global sensitivity analysis using Sobol’s method was extremely costly until high-performance computing became widespread in 2017.

Impact: Had 76 citations, providing a methodological framework for quantifying uncertainties in urban climate models. It promoted the evaluation of model credibility in climate-adaptive urban planning.

5. Planning Low-carbon Distributed Power Systems: Evaluating the Role of Energy Storage (2020)#

Description: Incorporated energy storage systems (with dynamic charge-discharge efficiency models) into power capacity expansion planning frameworks, evaluating the economic and technical value of energy storage in decarbonizing distributed power systems.

Why couldn’t it be done before: Early capacity planning models simplified energy storage as static devices, ignoring the dynamic behavior of charge-discharge efficiency with state changes. Lithium batteries hadn’t seen a significant cost reduction until 2020, so the commercial significance of incorporating energy storage into planning models was limited. With rapidly declining energy storage costs, the need for accurate modeling became urgent.

Impact: Had 46 citations, providing a tool for power system planners to quantify energy storage value. It has some application value in decision-making related to renewable energy absorption and grid decarbonization.
