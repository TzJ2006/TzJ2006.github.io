Breakthrough Works#

1. A new quantitative method to evaluate the spatial distribution of fibres in composites: the degree of randomness (2023)#

Description: Proposed a new quantitative metric called ‘degree of randomness’, which is used to assess the randomness of fiber distributions in composite materials, providing a measurable mathematical description of fiber distribution uniformity.

Why it couldn’t be done before: There was a lack of a unified quantitative framework for evaluating the randomness of fiber distributions. Researchers relied on visual judgment or indirect metrics such as nearest-neighbor distances, lacking a dimensionless metric that could be compared across different material systems. The key insight was to transfer the concept of randomness from statistical physics to the analysis of microstructures in composite materials.

Impact: Provided a standardized evaluation scale for evaluating algorithms that generate virtual microstructures. This directly supported an algorithm comparison work published in the same year, which received 6 citations.

2. Comparison of three algorithms generating virtual microstructures in terms of the degree of randomness (2023)#

Description: Based on the ‘degree of randomness’ metric, this paper systematically compares the performance of three algorithms that generate virtual microstructures in composite materials, evaluating their ability to reproduce realistic fiber distributions.

Why it couldn’t be done before: There was a lack of a unified evaluation criterion for comparing different algorithms for generating virtual microstructures. Each algorithm was developed independently and could not be compared across disciplines. The introduction of the ‘degree of randomness’ method made this systematic comparison possible.

Impact: Received 16 citations. This is the most influential work of the researcher so far, providing an algorithm selection reference for the community of composite material digital twins and multi-scale modeling.

3. Micromechanical modelling of unidirectional continuous fibre-reinforced composites: A review (2025)#

Description: A systematic review of micromechanical modeling methods for unidirectional continuous fiber-reinforced composites, outlining the evolution and current state of research in this field.

Why it couldn’t be done before: This is a review article written after the researcher established a quantitative method for microstructures in 2023. They had now acquired an overview of the entire micromechanical modeling field from a more macroscopic perspective.

Impact: Received 2 citations (published in 2025, with citation count still accumulating). It helps to integrate scattered literature on micromechanical modeling and provides a systematic reference for researchers new to this field.
