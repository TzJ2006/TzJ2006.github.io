3. Heat transfer characteristics of oscillating flow regenerator filled with circular tubes or parallel plates (2007)#

Description: An investigation of the heat transfer characteristics of oscillating flow regenerators — a core component of thermoacoustic systems. Cited 35 times, this is a foundational experimental study in thermoacoustic refrigeration and cryogenic engineering.

Impact: Provided experimental evidence for the optimized design of regenerators in thermoacoustic refrigerators and engines. The subsequent series of thermoacoustic engine papers from 2014–2015 (cited 27–30 times) all build upon this work.

4. Study of a liquid-piston traveling-wave thermoacoustic heat engine with different working gases (2014)#

Description: A study of the performance of a traveling-wave thermoacoustic heat engine using different working gases. Cited 30 times, this represents systematic experimental exploration of liquid-piston configurations in the field of thermoacoustic energy conversion.

Why it wasn’t possible before: Sealing and dynamic stability issues in liquid-piston thermoacoustic systems were difficult to address in early engineering implementations. Impact: Advanced the transition of thermoacoustic engines from laboratory concepts to engineering applications, particularly for potential use in low-grade waste heat recovery.
