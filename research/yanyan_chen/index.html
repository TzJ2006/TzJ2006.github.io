3. Heat transfer characteristics of oscillating flow regenerator filled with circular tubes or parallel plates (2007)#

Description: A study on the heat transfer characteristics of oscillating flow regenerators, which are core components of thermal acoustics systems. It was cited 35 times and is a basic experimental research in the fields of thermal acoustics refrigeration and cryogenic engineering.

Why couldn’t it be done before: The mechanism of oscillating flow heat transfer was complex, and early measuring instruments were not accurate enough to distinguish the heat transfer differences between circular tubes and parallel plates. By the early 2000s, the experimental platform for thermal acoustics systems became more mature, making precise parameterized research possible.

Impact: It provided an experimental basis for the optimization design of regenerators in thermal acoustics refrigeration machines and thermal engines. Subsequent papers on thermal engines from 2014-2015 (cited 27-30 times) were based on this work.

4. Study of a liquid-piston traveling-wave thermoacoustic heat engine with different working gases (2014)#

Description: A study on the performance of traveling-wave thermoacoustic engines using different working gases. It was cited 30 times and represents a systematic experimental exploration in the field of thermal acoustics energy conversion using liquid piston configurations.

Impact: It promoted the transformation of thermal engines from laboratory concepts to engineering applications, especially in the potential application areas of low-grade waste heat recovery.

5. An Evaluating Method of Public Transit Accessibility for Urban Areas Based on GIS (2016)#
