Breakthrough Works#

1. Spectroscopic and molecular modeling methods to study the interaction between naphthalimide-polyamine conjugates and DNA (2016)#

Description: Systematically investigated the interaction between naphthalimide-polyamine conjugates (compounds 1–7) and herring sperm DNA under physiological pH conditions, combining UV/fluorescence spectroscopy with molecular docking to elucidate how polyamine side-chain structure governs binding mode.

Why It Wasn’t Done Before: No systematic workflow existed for combining spectroscopic experimental data with in silico molecular docking, making it difficult to simultaneously quantify — from both experimental and theoretical perspectives — how side-chain length contributes to intercalation versus groove-binding modes.

Impact: Cited 34 times, making it the most-cited paper in this profile. It provided an experiment-computation joint analysis framework for structure optimization of naphthalimide derivatives as potential anticancer agents, advancing the subsequent design of polyamine-intercalator conjugate systems.

2. New bioactive secondary metabolites from the Anoectochilus roxburghii endophytic fungus Aspergillus versicolor (2020)#

Description: Isolated and identified 6 new compounds from the endophytic fungus Aspergillus versicolor of Anoectochilus roxburghii, including two novel isochroman lactones (versicoisochromanes A/B), two benzolactones, and a new sterol, with absolute configurations determined by NMR and ECD.

Why It Wasn’t Done Before: Secondary metabolite studies on endophytic fungi of Anoectochilus roxburghii were exceedingly rare, and the fungus’s unique chemical environment produced rare scaffolds. The widespread availability of high-resolution mass spectrometry and ECD calculations has significantly lowered the barrier to structural elucidation of trace novel compounds.

Impact: Cited 16 times. Expanded the known chemical diversity of the genus Aspergillus, with the newly discovered scaffolds contributing novel templates to the natural products library.

3. Hypersonins A-D, Polycyclic Polyprenylated Acylphloroglucinols with a 1,2-seco-Homoadamantane Architecture from Hypericum wilsonii (2020)#

Description: Isolated 4 polycyclic polyprenylated acylphloroglucinols (PPAPs) featuring a novel 1,2-seco-homoadamantane skeleton from Hypericum wilsonii, with the new scaffold type confirmed through comprehensive spectroscopic data, X-ray crystallography, and ECD calculations.

Why It Wasn’t Done Before: Structural elucidation of PPAPs relies on high-field NMR and computation-assisted absolute configuration determination (TDDFT/ECD), tools that only became mature and widely accessible in recent years. Deeper chemosystematic studies of the Hypericum genus also contributed to uncovering such rare skeletal architectures.

Impact: Cited 13 times. Reported the first naturally occurring PPAPs featuring a bicyclo[4.3.1]decane-3-methoxycarbonyl architecture, contributing a new scaffold type to the study of structural diversity in this compound family.

4. A Knowledge-Driven Cross-view Contrastive Learning for EEG Representation (2023)#

Description: Proposed a cross-view contrastive learning framework incorporating neurophysiological prior knowledge for unsupervised/self-supervised EEG representation learning, addressing the bottleneck of scarce labeled EEG data.

Why It Wasn’t Done Before: The challenge in EEG contrastive learning lies in constructing meaningful positive/negative sample pairs. Prior methods lacked mechanisms for systematically embedding neuroscientific domain knowledge (e.g., frequency bands, brain region topology) into contrastive learning frameworks, and the rise of large-scale pretraining paradigms is what made unsupervised EEG representation learning a viable direction.

Impact: This work (if by the same person) marks a significant shift in research direction, combining neuroscientific domain knowledge with modern self-supervised learning to offer new approaches for BCI applications constrained by limited labeled data.
