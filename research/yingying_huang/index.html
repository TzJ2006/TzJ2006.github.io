Impact: This paper received 34 citations, making it the researcher’s most cited paper. 2. New bioactive secondary metabolites from the Anoectochilus roxburghii endophytic fungus Aspergillus versicolor (2020)#

Why couldn’t this be done before: Research on secondary metabolites from endophytic fungi like Hypericum roxburghii was rare before. The unique chemical environment of these fungi led to the discovery of rare structures. High-resolution mass spectrometry and ECD calculations made it easier to identify new compounds.

Impact: This paper received 16 citations, expanding the known chemical diversity of the Aspergillus genus. The discovered new structures provided a new framework for natural product research.

3. Hypersonins A-D, Polycyclic Polyprenylated Acylphloroglucinols with a 1,2-seco-Homoadamantane Architecture from Hypericum wilsonii (2020)#

Description: Four new polycyclic polyprenylated acylphloroglucinols with a 1,2-seco-adamantane structure were isolated from Hypericum wilsonii. These structures were confirmed using spectroscopy, X-ray crystallography, and ECD calculations.

4. A Knowledge-Driven Cross-view Contrastive Learning for EEG Representation (2023)#

Description: This paper proposed a cross-view contrastive learning framework that combined neural physiological knowledge to perform unsupervised/self-supervised representation learning on EEG signals. This approach helped solve the problem of scarcity of labeled EEG data.

Impact: This work represents a significant shift in research direction. It combines neuroscience knowledge with modern self-supervised learning techniques, providing new ideas for BCI applications where data efficiency is limited.
