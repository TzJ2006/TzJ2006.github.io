Research Track#

It should be noted that there is a serious issue of confusion regarding the names of researchers in this paper list. “Wu Lin” is a common Chinese name, and the database aggregates the achievements of at least 5 researchers with similar names from different fields: ① Plant Soil Ecologist (Continuous cropping obstacles/rhizosphere microbiome, 2001-2025, forming the core main line); ② Computer Vision Researcher (Pedestrian recognition, 2018+); ③ Quantum Photonic Physicist (2018+); ④ Machine Learning Optimization Researcher (Natural gradients, KFAC, 2021+); ⑤ Radio Astronomer (2019+). The following analysis focuses on the main researcher with a complete academic context, concentrated citation volume, and coherent theme—Wu Lin in the field of plant rhizosphere microecology.

The core academic contribution of this researcher lies in systematically revealing the rhizosphere microecological mechanisms of continuous cropping obstacles in Chinese herbal medicines. His academic career has gone through three clear stages: The first stage (2001-2011) started from traditional agriculture, where he was the first to introduce metaproteomics into rhizospheric soil research. Using Rehmannia glutinosa as a model system, he established a technical platform for studying continuous cropping obstacles. The second stage (2013-2018) focused on mechanism analysis. Through chemical analysis of root exudates using HPLC, high-throughput sequencing (pyrosequencing, Illumina), and metagenomics, he constructed a causal chain of “phenolic acid accumulation → pathogenic microorganism proliferation → rhizosphere microbial community imbalance → continuous cropping reduction in yield.” The third stage (2018-2022) shifted to intervention strategies, exploring the effects of bioorganic fertilizers and intercropping systems on continuous cropping obstacles. This marked a transition from mechanism research to application-oriented studies.

Breakthrough Works#

1. Characterization of metaproteomics in crop rhizospheric soil (2011)#

Description: For the first time, metaproteomics technology was systematically applied to crop rhizospheric soil research. Functional microbial proteins were directly identified in rhizospheric soil, revealing the actual metabolic activities of rhizospheric microbial communities, rather than relying solely on the “potential presence” information of 16S rRNA genes.

Why it couldn’t be done before: Technical bottleneck: The rhizospheric soil matrix was complex, and humic acids caused serious interference. Protein extraction and mass spectrometry identification were not yet mature before 2011. Additionally, the database had insufficient coverage of soil microbial protein sequences, limiting the depth of identification. The maturity of high-throughput mass spectrometry and bioinformatics processes made this breakthrough possible.

Impact: It pioneered the research direction of metaproteomics in Chinese agricultural soils. The paper was cited 130 times and provided a core methodological framework for subsequent studies on continuous cropping obstacles. It promoted the transformation of the field from community structure description to functional analysis.

2. Plant-microbe rhizosphere interactions mediated by Rehmannia glutinosa root exudates under consecutive monoculture (2015)#

Description: Through four years of field experiments combined with laboratory verification, it was demonstrated that phenolic substances secreted by the roots of Rehmannia glutinosa had a cumulative effect under sterile conditions. This directly led to an imbalance in the rhizospheric microbial community structure, and key pathogenic secondary metabolites were identified. A “root exudate-microorganism-plant health” triple interaction model was established.

Why it couldn’t be done before: Lack of key insights: Previous studies attributed continuous cropping obstacles to soil nutrient depletion or single pathogens, lacking a systematic understanding of plant-microbial chemical interactions. The long-term experimental accumulation required for four years of field trials and the popularization of HPLC-mass spectrometry technology were prerequisites.

Impact: The paper was cited 135 times and became a landmark paper in the research field of continuous cropping obstacles. It provided a complete biochemical evidence chain for continuous cropping obstacles in Rehmannia glutinosa, promoting sustainable cultivation of Chinese medicinal materials as an independent research direction.

3. The role of organic acids on microbial deterioration in the Radix pseudostellariae rhizosphere under continuous monoculture regimes (2017)#

Description: Using Radix pseudostellariae as the subject, through three years of field trials combined with transcriptome sequencing, it was explained at the molecular level how organic acids (oxalic acid, succinic acid, etc.) selectively promoted the proliferation of pathogenic fungi (Talaromyces helicus, Kosakonia sacchari), revealing the targeted microbial regulation mechanism of phenolic substances.

Why it couldn’t be done before: The cost of transcriptome sequencing decreased significantly after 2015, making it possible to analyze gene expression responses to organic acids. The results of pathogen identification from previous studies (Frontiers in Microbiology, 2016) provided a clear research target for this study.

Impact: The paper was cited 94 times and advanced the research on continuous cropping obstacles to the gene regulation level, providing a theoretical basis for targeted biocontrol strategies (such as selecting antagonistic microorganisms that can degrade specific organic acids).

4. Belowground Interactions Impact the Soil Bacterial Community, Soil Fertility, and Crop Yield in Maize/Peanut Intercropping Systems (2018)#

Description: Using a physical isolation experimental design (non-isolation/semi-isolation with nylon net/complete isolation with plastic film), it quantified the independent contributions of root contact, root exudate exchange, and competitive nutrient absorption to the rhizosphere community in maize/peanut intercropping systems. Different physical pathways of rhizosphere interaction were isolated for the first time.

Why it couldn’t be done before: Experimental design innovation: Previous intercropping studies could not distinguish between different physical mechanisms of root interactions. This three-treatment isolation design was a key methodological breakthrough. Combined with the reduced cost of 16S amplicon sequencing, large-scale sample analysis became possible.

Impact: The paper was cited 106 times and provided a mechanism basis for optimizing intercropping systems. It promoted the development of the concept of “designed rhizosphere microecology” and had direct guiding value for sustainable agricultural practices.

5. Antagonistic Activity of Trichoderma spp. Against Fusarium oxysporum in Rhizosphere of Radix pseudostellariae Triggers the Expression of Host Defense Genes and Improves Its Growth Under Long-Term Monoculture System (2021)#

Description: It was proven that Trichoderma spp. not only directly antagonistically inhibits Fusarium oxysporum but also activates the expression of host defense genes in Rehmannia glutinosa, achieving an “indirect immune activation” effect. This marked a transition from passive mechanism analysis to active intervention verification.

Why it couldn’t be done before: It required integrating interdisciplinary capabilities between macro-agricultural experiments and molecular plant pathology analysis. Effective biocontrol experiments could only be designed after identifying the pathogen (Fusarium oxysporum, 2015, 2017).

Impact: The paper was cited 53 times and provided direct application evidence for biological control strategies of continuous cropping obstacles. It marked a new stage in the researcher’s transformation from mechanism research to green agricultural solutions.

Methodological Evolution#

The researcher’s methodological evolution showed clear technical-driven characteristics. In the early stage (2001-2011), traditional microbial culture techniques and two-dimensional electrophoresis were used. In 2011, the introduction of metaproteomics was a first technological leap, marking a shift from “culturing-dependent” to “culturing-independent” research paradigms. From 2013 to 2016, with the popularization of pyrosequencing, researchers widely used 16S rRNA gene amplicon sequencing combined with CLPP (community level physiological maps) to describe community structures. At the same time, HPLC was introduced for quantitative analysis of root exudates, enabling correlation analysis between “microbial community changes” and “chemical driving factors.” Since 2017, the introduction of transcriptome sequencing enabled research to go deeper into gene regulation, revealing the molecular mechanisms of microorganisms in response to chemical signals. From 2018 to 2022, the methodology became more integrated: metagenomics (functional potential prediction) was combined with field agricultural indicators (yield, enzyme activity, soil nutrients), forming a “mechanism-effect” dual-validation system. Throughout the process, researchers always adhered to the dual-track model of “field verification + laboratory mechanism analysis,” avoiding the problem of disconnection between pure genomics research and actual agriculture.

Domain Impact#

Wu Lin made foundational contributions to the field of rhizosphere ecology of continuous cropping obstacles in Chinese herbal medicines. His impact is reflected at two levels: Methodological level, where he was the first to introduce modern genomics technologies such as metaproteomics and metagenomics into the study of Chinese medicinal materials’ rhizospheres, providing a technical demonstration for the entire field. At the mechanism level, the causal chain of “root exudates (phenolic acids/organic acids) → rhizosphere microbial imbalance → continuous cropping reduction in yield” has become a consensus in the field and has been cited and extended by many subsequent studies. Considering that his h-index is 26 and he has received 2,130 citations, but only 125 of them were from the past five years, his academic influence peaked between 2011 and 2018. His core contributions have been fully absorbed by the field. His research directly serves the issue of sustainable cultivation of traditional Chinese medicinal materials, having clear industrial application directions beyond academic value. It has promoted the application of bioorganic fertilizers and intercropping systems in the production of Chinese medicinal materials.
