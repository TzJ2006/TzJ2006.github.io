Research Trajectory#

The “Fan Chen” in this dataset most likely represents merged records from multiple researchers sharing the same name, rather than the complete trajectory of a single scholar. Based on the paper content, at least two distinctly different researchers can be identified: (1) a researcher with a long-term focus on analytical chemistry/food chemistry, spanning 1992–2022, evolving from early forensic gas chromatography to ionic liquid extraction and natural deep eutectic solvents (NADES); and (2) a large number of computer science papers appearing after 2019 (reinforcement learning, ReRAM accelerators, quantum computing, optimization theory, diffusion models, etc.), whose topics are entirely unrelated to the former, clearly originating from different researchers sharing the same name.

Focusing on the traceable analytical chemistry strand, the academic trajectory is clear: beginning with forensic toxicology gas chromatography detection during 1992–2008, transitioning to HPLC-DAD food/pharmaceutical fingerprinting in the 2010s, then achieving the first high-impact breakthrough in 2015–2016 by introducing ionic liquids into liquid-phase microextraction and countercurrent chromatography, with publications in Food Chemistry and J. Chromatography A garnering 85 and 61 citations respectively. From 2019–2021, the researcher expanded into natural deep eutectic solvent (NADES) green extraction, publishing multiple highly cited papers in succession and establishing a strong academic presence in the green analytical chemistry niche.

The h-index of 12, total citations of 542, and the proportion of 287 citations in the past five years reflect a mid-career researcher with steadily growing influence in a specific niche (ionic liquid/DES green sample pretreatment), rather than a broad cross-disciplinary figure.

Breakthrough Works#

1. Determination of chlorophenols in honey samples using in-situ ionic liquid-dispersive liquid-liquid microextraction as a pretreatment method followed by high-performance liquid chromatography (2015)#

Description: Proposed an in-situ ionic liquid dispersive liquid-liquid microextraction (IL-DLLME) method, in which a hydrophilic ionic liquid [C4MIM][BF4] undergoes anion exchange to in-situ generate the hydrophobic extractant [C4MIM][NTf2], enabling enrichment and HPLC detection of six chlorophenols in honey, with 85 citations.

Why It Wasn’t Done Before: Conventional DLLME relied on toxic organic solvents (chlorobenzene, carbon tetrachloride, etc.) as extractants; complex food matrices contain abundant interfering substances, and the strategy of in-situ generating ionic liquids had not been systematically applied to trace contaminant analysis in honey prior to this work. Moreover, the significant reduction in commercial ionic liquid costs in the late 2010s was what made the approach practically viable.

Impact: Opened a practical pathway for ionic liquid microextraction in food safety, with numerous subsequent papers extending this approach to complex matrices such as wine and vegetable oil, establishing this work as a landmark paradigm paper in the field.

2. Determination of Alternaria mycotoxins in wine and juice using ionic liquid modified countercurrent chromatography as a pretreatment method followed by high-performance liquid chromatography (2016)#

Description: Introduced ionic liquids into the countercurrent chromatography (CCC) solvent system, enabling preparative-scale enrichment and purification of Alternaria mycotoxins in wine and fruit juice, published in J. Chromatography A with 61 citations.

Why It Wasn’t Done Before: CCC traditionally relied on standard solvent systems such as hexane/ethyl acetate/methanol/water, offering limited selectivity for polar mycotoxins. The tunable polarity of ionic liquids had not been systematically integrated into CCC phase system design prior to 2015, and insufficient purity of early ionic liquids would cause unstable phase separation.

Impact: Established the viability of ionic liquid-modified CCC as a preparative tool for natural products and food contaminants, inspiring a series of related studies on lipid separation and anthocyanin purification.

3. Selective microextraction of polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons using a hydrophobic deep eutectic solvent composed with an iron oxide-based nanoferrofluid (2019)#

Description: Combined a hydrophobic deep eutectic solvent (DES) with an iron oxide-based nanoferrofluid to achieve magnetically assisted selective microextraction of polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons, published in Microchimica Acta with 40 citations.

Why It Wasn’t Done Before: Hydrophobic NADES were not systematically synthesized and characterized until around 2017. Combining DES with magnetic nanoparticles for “magnetic DLLME” required simultaneous expertise in both DES design and nanofluid preparation — a cross-disciplinary integration that was rarely explored before 2019.

Impact: Advanced the application of DES in environmental contaminant detection; the dual advantage of green solvent and magnetic separation was widely adopted by subsequent research.

4. A priori design of new natural deep eutectic solvent for lutein recovery from microalgae (2021)#

Description: Established an a priori design framework based on intermolecular hydrogen bond interactions, systematically screening NADES for green extraction of lutein from microalgae, published in Food Chemistry with 74 citations.

Why It Wasn’t Done Before: Prior NADES research largely relied on trial-and-error experiments, lacking rational design methods for predicting solubility based on molecular structure. The combined application of molecular dynamics simulation and Hansen solubility parameters to NADES design for food extraction only matured in the early 2020s.

Impact: Shifted NADES research from empirical synthesis toward mechanism-driven a priori design, providing a methodological paradigm for green extraction chemistry with direct implications for microalgae biorefining.

5. A Theoretical Study on Terpene‐Based Natural Deep Eutectic Solvent: Relationship between Viscosity and Hydrogen‐Bonding Interactions (2021)#

Description: Through quantum chemical calculations and molecular dynamics simulations, elucidated the microscopic mechanism underlying the low viscosity of terpene-based NADES — specifically, the quantitative relationship between hydrogen bond network strength and viscosity — published in Global Challenges with 72 citations.

Why It Wasn’t Done Before: High viscosity is a fundamental barrier to the industrialization of NADES, but its microscopic origins had long remained unclear. Terpene-based low-viscosity systems were not systematically studied before 2019, and systematically correlating DFT calculations with rheological measurements required interdisciplinary integration of computational and analytical chemistry.

Impact: Theoretically explained the viscosity advantage of terpene-based NADES, laying thermodynamic and kinetic foundations for the rational design of low-viscosity green solvents and facilitating their translation into practical separation applications.

Research Directions#

  • Design, characterization, and green extraction applications of natural deep eutectic solvents (NADES)
  • Chromatographic method development for forensic and food analysis (GC, HPLC, LC-MS)
  • Preparative-scale separation and purification of bioactive natural products

Methodological Evolution#

The methodological evolution of this analytical chemist follows a clear three-stage progression. The first stage (1992–2008) centered on gas chromatography (GC/FPD, GC-MS) as the primary tool, focusing on quantitative detection of forensic toxins (TETS) and food components, with methods predominantly targeting single analytes in single matrices and limited technical depth. The second stage (2010–2014) shifted toward simultaneous multi-component determination by HPLC-DAD/ELSD, with attempts at constructing traditional Chinese medicine fingerprint profiles, marking a transition toward more complex analytical systems — though technical novelty remained limited.

The third stage (2015–present) represents a fundamental paradigm shift: the researcher introduced ionic liquids (ILs) and deep eutectic solvents (DES/NADES) into sample pretreatment, moving the core innovation from “detection methods themselves” to “green, efficient sample enrichment/separation strategies.” This transition aligned with the broader trend of “green analytical chemistry,” elevating the work from technical application to methodological innovation, with a corresponding significant increase in citation counts. More recently (2021), the integration of quantum chemical calculations and molecular dynamics simulations further elevated empirical solvent design to mechanism-driven rational design, marking a higher-level evolution toward computationally assisted experimentation.

Field Impact#

Within the analytical chemistry niche of green sample pretreatment methods, this researcher is one of the key contributors to ionic liquid-countercurrent chromatography coupling and natural deep eutectic solvent extraction. The representative papers (Food Chemistry 2015, J. Chromatography A 2016, and the 2021 NADES series) together account for approximately 470 citations — 87% of total citations — indicating that impact is heavily concentrated in the green pretreatment method development phase of 2015–2021. This researcher’s work advanced NADES from proof-of-concept to practical applications in food safety and natural product separation, though no paradigm-shifting breakthroughs that redefine the broader field of analytical chemistry have emerged; influence remains confined to the green analytical methods niche. It should be noted that the numerous computer science papers (reinforcement learning, quantum computing, optimization algorithms, etc.) appearing after 2019 in the publication list originate from other researchers sharing the same name and should not be attributed to this analytical chemistry researcher.
