Research Trajectory#

L. Teng’s academic trajectory is highly fragmented, with publications spanning completely unrelated fields — glioma treatment, cardiac stem cells, NADPH oxidase vascular biology, marine model organism immunology, and GaAs quantum well spin relaxation — strongly suggesting that this name aggregates multiple distinct researchers (a name disambiguation problem). Focusing on the primary research thread, one can identify a core body of work centered on glioma diagnosis and treatment: the field was entered in 2011 with the landmark review Aberrant Signaling Pathways in Glioma (219 citations), followed by a series of high-impact mechanistic studies on 5-ALA fluorescence-guided surgery and photodynamic therapy between 2011 and 2013, which constitute the main source of academic influence.

The pivotal turning point in this career trajectory was the concentrated burst of output during 2011–2013 — by elucidating the regulatory mechanisms of ferrochelatase silencing and ABCB6 overexpression on protoporphyrin IX accumulation, this work provided molecular targets for improving clinical 5-ALA fluorescence surgery quality, with three representative papers accumulating over 450 citations combined. However, only 63 citations in the past five years (out of 1,607 lifetime) indicate that research impact has declined markedly since 2018, with new output increasingly scattered across topics (C-phycocyanin, zebrafish, SGLT-2) without establishing a sustained core research program.

Breakthrough Works#

1. Aberrant Signaling Pathways in Glioma (2011)#

Description: A systematic review of aberrant activation mechanisms of key signaling pathways in glioblastoma (GBM) — including EGFR, PDGFR, PTEN/PI3K/Akt, Rb/CDK4, and p53 — providing a theoretical framework for targeted therapeutic strategies.

Why this wasn’t done before: Accumulation of the key insights took time: only after the 2007 TCGA glioma genomic atlas data release could researchers systematically integrate the full picture of multi-pathway aberrations; prior to this, individual pathway studies were relatively isolated, lacking a cross-pathway integrative perspective.

Impact: Became a highly cited review in the glioma signaling pathway field (219 citations), providing a standard reference framework for subsequent targeted drug development and combination therapy strategies.

2. Silencing of ferrochelatase enhances 5-aminolevulinic acid-based fluorescence and photodynamic therapy efficacy (2011)#

Description: Discovered that silencing ferrochelatase (FECH) blocks the metabolic conversion of PpIX to heme, thereby significantly enhancing both fluorescence intensity and photodynamic therapy efficacy of 5-ALA in glioma cells.

Why this wasn’t done before: 5-ALA had been used clinically for fluorescence-guided surgery for years, but insufficient PpIX accumulation limited fluorescence quality. The breakthrough lay in identifying FECH as the key metabolic bottleneck — which required a detailed enzymatic map of the 5-ALA metabolic pathway and mature gene silencing technology (siRNA), both of which became simultaneously available around 2010.

Impact: Cited 158 times, pioneering the research direction of improving 5-ALA fluorescence quality through metabolic intervention and providing an actionable target for optimizing clinical fluorescence-guided surgery.

3. Increased Expression of ABCB6 Enhances Protoporphyrin IX Accumulation and Photodynamic Effect in Human Glioma (2013)#

Description: Revealed that overexpression of the ATP-binding cassette transporter ABCB6 promotes PpIX accumulation in mitochondria, enhancing photodynamic therapy sensitivity in glioma through a complementary mechanism.

Why this wasn’t done before: The functional role of ABCB6 in porphyrin transport was only preliminarily established in 2009; connecting it to glioma PDT sensitization required cross-disciplinary integration (mitochondrial transport biology + tumor photodynamic therapy), a connection that had not previously been made.

Impact: Cited 98 times, together with the ferrochelatase study establishing a dual-target strategy for regulating PpIX metabolism, driving a wave of mechanistic research in glioma photodynamic therapy.

4. The Heme Oxygenase 1 Inducer (CoPP) Protects Human Cardiac Stem Cells against Apoptosis through Activation of the Extracellular Signal-regulated Kinase (ERK)/NRF2 Signaling Pathway and Cytokine Release (2012)#

Description: First demonstrated that the HO-1 inducer CoPP can enhance post-transplant survival of human cardiac stem cells (hCSCs) through the ERK/NRF2 pathway and cytokine secretion, addressing the core obstacle of massive transplanted cell death in stem cell therapy.

Why this wasn’t done before: The clinical translational potential of c-kit+ cardiac stem cells was only demonstrated in 2003, and their extremely low post-transplant survival rate (>90% dying within 7 days) had become a bottleneck. Applying the cytoprotective mechanisms of the HO-1/CO system to cardiac stem cell preconditioning required systematic integration of knowledge from two independent fields (HO-1 cardiovascular protection and stem cell therapy).

Impact: Cited 97 times, establishing the paradigm of a “preconditioning + stem cell transplantation” combined strategy and advancing the field of cell survival research in cardiac regenerative medicine.

5. The synergistic antitumor effects of all-trans retinoic acid and C-phycocyanin on the lung cancer A549 cells in vitro and in vivo (2015)#

Description: Demonstrated in both in vitro and in vivo models that the combination of all-trans retinoic acid (ATRA) and C-phycocyanin exerts synergistic antitumor effects against lung cancer A549 cells, providing experimental evidence for combining natural products with differentiation-inducing therapy.

Why this wasn’t done before: The antitumor mechanisms of C-phycocyanin were only gradually elucidated in the early 2010s; combining it with the established ATRA differentiation therapy strategy required cross-mechanistic pathway research, and sufficient data accumulation to support systematic combination studies was only available around 2015.

Impact: Cited 86 times, one of the landmark studies in the field of C-phycocyanin antitumor applications, advancing the exploration of this natural pigment in tumor pharmacology.

  • Cardiac stem cells and myocardial protection (HO-1/NO preconditioning strategies, ERK/NRF2 survival pathways)

Methodological Evolution#

Early work (2008–2010) relied primarily on classical molecular biology techniques: western blot, gene knockout mouse models, and protein phosphorylation analysis, mainly investigating the regulatory mechanisms of NADPH oxidase. The 2011–2014 period represented methodological maturity, incorporating RNAi gene silencing, fluorescence microscopy, and in vitro/in vivo photodynamic therapy models; experimental design shifted from single-molecule mechanisms toward full-chain validation of “target → intervention → functional phenotype,” driving the production of the highest-impact papers. Post-2015, the methodology became notably fragmented: while in vitro/in vivo tumor models continued, zebrafish developmental models, phylogenetic analysis, and reproductive toxicology assays were introduced — entirely different methodological frameworks — reflecting a lack of sustained research focus. In recent years (2024–2025), the work has shifted toward clinical observational research, with published cardiology clinical reviews bearing almost no methodological continuity with the earlier basic research.

Field Impact#

In the specialized subfield of glioma 5-ALA metabolic regulation, L. Teng (as the glioma research entity) made substantive contributions: through the discovery of two complementary mechanistic targets — ferrochelatase and ABCB6 — the work systematically elucidated the metabolic network governing PpIX accumulation, and has been frequently cited by subsequent research in the field; the h-index of 21 is primarily supported by this period. However, because the main research thread lost focus after 2017, a sustained research program around 5-ALA surgery optimization or glioma-targeted therapy was never established, missing the window to translate early mechanistic discoveries into clinical applications. Overall assessment: an effective contributor to a specific subfield, rather than a leading figure in the broader domain; the sharp decline in impact over the past five years (only 63 citations) indicates that academic influence has entered a plateau.
