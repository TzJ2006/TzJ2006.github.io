Research Trajectory#

L. Teng’s academic trajectory is highly fragmented, with papers covering a wide range of fields including glioma treatment, cardiac stem cells, NADPH oxidase vascular biology, immunology in marine model organisms, and spin relaxation in GaAs quantum wells—all areas that are not related to each other. This strongly suggests that this researcher identity is actually the result of collaboration between multiple researchers (a problem of merging with the same name). If we focus on his main research lines, we can identify a research entity centered around glioma treatment and diagnosis: In 2011, he published a review article titled “Aberrant Signaling Pathways in Glioma” which received 219 citations. Between 2011 and 2013, he published several influential papers on mechanisms related to 5-ALA fluorescence-guided surgery and photodynamic therapy, which constituted the main source of his academic influence.

The key turning point in his career was during the intensive period from 2011 to 2013—through revealing the regulatory mechanism between iron chelators and ABCB6 overexpression, he provided a molecular target for improving the quality of clinical 5-ALA fluorescence surgery. His three representative papers received over 450 citations. However, his citations in the past five years were only 63 (out of 1,607 lifetime citations), indicating that his research influence has significantly declined after 2018. His new directions are becoming more dispersed (C-phycocyanin, zebrafish, SGLT-2), and he has not established a continuous core research program.

Breakthrough Works#

1. Aberrant Signaling Pathways in Glioma (2011)#

Description: A systematic review of the abnormal activation mechanisms of key signaling pathways such as EGFR, PDGFR, PTEN/PI3K/Akt, Rb/CDK4, and p53 in glioblastoma multiforme, providing a theoretical framework for targeted treatment strategies.

Why couldn’t it be done before: The accumulation of key insights required time: After the release of TCGA glioma genome data in 2007, researchers were able to systematically integrate the full picture of multi-pathway abnormalities. Prior research on each pathway was relatively isolated, lacking a cross-pathway integration perspective.

Impact: It became a highly cited review in the field of glioma signaling pathways (219 citations), providing a standard reference framework for subsequent targeted drug development and combination therapy strategies.

2. Silencing of ferrochelatase enhances 5-aminolevulinic acid-based fluorescence and photodynamic therapy efficacy (2011)#

Description: It was found that silencing iron chelators could block the metabolism from PpIX to hemoglobin, thereby significantly enhancing the fluorescence intensity of 5-ALA in glioma cells and improving the effectiveness of photodynamic therapy.

Why couldn’t it be done before: Clinical 5-ALA fluorescence surgery has been used for many years, but insufficient PpIX accumulation limited the quality of fluorescence. The breakthrough of this work was identifying FECH as a key metabolic bottleneck—both detailed enzyme maps of the 5-ALA metabolism pathway and mature siRNA technology were developed around 2010.

Impact: It received 158 citations, pioneering the research direction of improving 5-ALA fluorescence quality through metabolic intervention, providing an operational target for optimizing clinical fluorescence-guided surgery.

3. Increased Expression of ABCB6 Enhances Protoporphyrin IX Accumulation and Photodynamic Effect in Human Glioma (2013)#

Description: It was revealed that overexpression of ATP-binding cassette transporter ABCB6 could promote the accumulation of PpIX in mitochondria, enhancing the sensitivity of glioma to photodynamic therapy from another mechanism.

Why couldn’t it be done before: The function of ABCB6 in porphyrin transport was only initially understood in 2009. Connecting it with the enhancement of glioma PDT required cross-disciplinary knowledge integration (mitochondrial transport biology + tumor photodynamic therapy), which had not been completed before.

Impact: It received 98 citations, together with iron chelators research, constructing a dual-target strategy for regulating PpIX metabolism, driving a wave of research on the mechanism of glioma photodynamic therapy.

4. The Heme Oxygenase 1 Inducer (CoPP) Protects Human Cardiac Stem Cells against Apoptosis through Activation of the Extracellular Signal-regulated Kinase (ERK)/NRF2 Signaling Pathway and Cytokine Release (2012)#

Description: It was demonstrated for the first time that HO-1 inducer CoPP could enhance the survival rate of human cardiac stem cells after transplantation by activating the ERK/NRF2 pathway and cytokine release. This solved the core problem of high cell death rates during stem cell transplantation in clinical treatment.

Why couldn’t it be done before: The clinical potential of c-kit+ cardiac stem cells was only confirmed in 2003, and their extremely low survival rate (more than 90% died within 7 days) became a bottleneck. Applying the cellular protection mechanism of HO-1/CO system to cardiac stem cell pre-treatment required systematic integration of knowledge from two independent fields (HO-1 cardiovascular protection and stem cell therapy).

Impact: It received 97 citations, establishing the paradigm of “preparation + stem cell transplantation” combination strategy, promoting research on cell survival in cardiac regeneration medicine.

5. The synergistic antitumor effects of all-trans retinoic acid and C-phycocyanin on the lung cancer A549 cells in vitro and in vivo (2015)#

Description: In both in vitro and in vivo models, it was confirmed that the combined use of all-trans retinoic acid and C-phycocyanin had a synergistic antitumor effect on lung cancer A549 cells, providing experimental evidence for the combination of natural products with differentiation induction therapy.

Why couldn’t it be done before: The anti-tumor mechanism of C-phycocyanin was gradually understood in the early 2010s. Combining it with mature ATRA differentiation treatment required cross-structural research on both mechanisms, and data accumulation was only sufficient for systematic combined research around 2015.

Impact: It received 86 citations, making it one of the landmark studies in the field of C-phycocyanin anti-tumor applications, promoting the exploration of this natural pigment in tumor pharmacology.

Methodological Evolution#

Early stages (2008-2010) relied mainly on classical molecular biology methods: western blot, gene knockout mouse models, protein phosphorylation analysis, focusing primarily on the regulatory mechanism of NADPH oxidase. From 2011 to 2014, methodology matured with the introduction of RNAi gene silencing, fluorescence microscopy imaging, and in vitro photodynamic therapy models. Experimental design shifted from single-molecule mechanisms to a full-chain verification of “target → intervention → functional phenotype,” leading to the production of the most influential papers. After 2015, methodology became significantly fragmented: on one hand, in vivo tumor models continued; on the other hand, completely different methods such as zebrafish developmental models, systematic developmental analysis, and reproductive toxicology testing were introduced, indicating a lack of continuous focus in research directions. In recent years (2024-2025), clinical observational studies have been pursued, with cardiac clinical reviews published, showing little methodological continuity compared to early basic research.

Domain Impact#

In the sub-field of glioma 5-ALA metabolism regulation, L. Teng made substantial contributions as the main researcher in glioma research: Through the discovery of two complementary mechanism targets, iron chelators and ABCB6, he systematically clarified the metabolic network that affects PpIX accumulation, which was frequently cited by subsequent research in this field. His h-index of 21 mainly came from this period. However, due to the loss of focus on his main research lines after 2017, he failed to establish a continuous research program around 5-ALA surgery optimization or glioma targeted therapy, missing the window to advance early mechanism discoveries into clinical translation. Overall assessment: He is an effective contributor in specific sub-fields, not a leading figure in the overall domain; his significant decline in influence over the past five years (only 63 citations) indicates that his academic influence has entered a plateau phase.
