Learnings#

Domain Knowledge (domain)#

  • In cross-section tasks in spatial transcriptomics, the comparability of embedding spaces (shared coordinate system) is more critical than local embedding quality. Foundation models (scGPT/TEDDY) are naturally suited for cross-section retrieval due to their fixed representation spaces; PCA/STAIG independent processing produces incomparable coordinate systems and is not suitable for cross-section tasks.
  • The optimal method for single-section clustering (STAIG) and the optimal method for cross-section retrieval (scGPT) are different; different encoders should be selected for different task objectives, and a unified optimal solution should not be pursued.
  • TEDDY-G (rank-value encoding) outperforms TEDDY-X (binned expression); linear probe far outperforms fine-tuning (RA: 0.56→0.93), indicating high embedding quality but susceptibility to overfitting during fine-tuning; 8% higher than Nicheformer on held-out donor multi-class classification.

Tools (tools)#

  • Single-cell foundation models in the HuggingFace ecosystem (TEDDY, scGPT, etc.) have significantly different forward interfaces; standard HuggingFace interfaces cannot be assumed. Source code must be read to confirm the gene_ids parameter name and output dict structure. TEDDY’s vocab uses Ensembl IDs (43808 tokens) rather than gene symbols.

Architecture (architecture)#

  • TEDDY uses rank-value encoding, retaining the top 2048 genes as a token sequence. The sequence length is longer than models like scGPT, resulting in higher VRAM requirements and the need for smaller batch sizes during inference.
