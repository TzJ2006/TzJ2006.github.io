Problems & Solutions#

Critical Issues#

1. In HD immune analysis, T cell markers were found to be extremely sparse (1278/17502 spots, average 0.014), and TLS markers were even sparser (242 spots). The original argument that ‘multi-modal captures immune infiltration better than single-modal’ did not hold.#

Solution: Modified the narrative in R6 P3 from ‘multi-modal identification of immune infiltration boundaries’ to ‘clustering-retrieval balance’. 96% of STAIG retrieval results were concentrated in the epithelial domain (overhomogenization). PCA gene expression showed 3.8x higher T cell enrichment than STAIG’s 2.2x. Attention fusion performed most evenly at the boundary (60/40 Epithelium/CT). Key Insight: Frozen FM embedding’s optimal clustering strategy (spatial graph contrast learning) and retrieval strategy (attention fusion) are different. This is a more meaningful discovery than expected.

General Issues#

2. The benchmark_rm_ideal.py defaults to writing results to the same summary.csv file. The results of each new pair of experiments will overwrite those of the previous pair.#

Solution: Back up the results immediately after each pair of experiments (e.g., summary_151673_151674.csv). The results from 151673↔151669 were manually reconstructed using heredoc (since they were overwritten by the results of 151674). Key Insight: Long-running batch experiments require per-pair output directories to be designed at the script level, or the results should be aggregated after the pipeline is completed.

3. conda activate does not produce any output in the background commands. The output files of the three experimental processes (bgqoqyepm, etc.) are always 0 bytes.#

Solution: Use [[[HYMTPH_1]] instead of [[[HYMTPH_2]] to ensure proper background output writing. Key Insight: The background bash command environment of Claude Code does not inherit the shell’s activate state. Condna run must be used to ensure environment isolation.

4. The same experiment was started twice with two different conda activate and conda run versions. Running 6 benchmark processes simultaneously wastes resources.#

Solution: Use ps aux to find the PID of the conda run process and kill the duplicate 3 processes. Keep the original conda activate version. Key Insight: When the background process output is empty, it is necessary to confirm whether the process is actually running (ps aux) before submitting again.

5. In the HD Niche Query visualization, the query points (red stars) are not clearly visible, making them unusable for paper presentation.#

Solution: Fixed the script: increased the star size from markersize=20 to 26, added a white background marker (size=32), marked all 4 panels, and added arrows and text labels to the first panel. Key Insight: In the paper figure, the query spot requires multiple visual cues (color, size, outer circle, label). A single red star is completely hidden behind the heatmap background.

AI Limitations#

General Limitations#

  • After generating visual charts, there was no active verification of the quality (such as query point visibility). Users needed feedback to identify problems. There was also a lack of proactive QA regarding image usability.
  • The issue of benchmark_rm_ideal.py overwriting summary.csv was not detected in advance. As a result, the results from 151673↔151669 were overwritten. It was necessary to manually reconstruct the data using heredoc based on memory.

Learnings#

Key Learnings#

  • It was discovered that there are two possible optimal fusion strategies between clustering and retrieval: STAIG’s spatial graph contrast learning is most suitable for global domain clustering (Average ARI 0.546), but its strong spatial homogenization characteristics lead to excessive focus on the same domain at the boundary (96% Epithelium). Attention fusion (60/40 distribution) performs more evenly at the boundary microenvironment retrieval. This is the new core argument in R6.
  • Results of the three new RM-IDEAL experiments: For the same donor across slices (151673↔151674), the Spearman ρ for Layer 3 was as high as 0.86. For the same donor (151508↔151509), the same_label@50 reached 100%. Across donors (151673↔151669), a negative correlation appeared in Layer 3 (ρ=-0.73), indicating systematic differences in the morphological structure of Layer 3 between different donors.
  • In HD colon cancer data, T cell markers (CD3D/CD3E, etc.) were extremely sparse at 8μm spots (7.3% expressed). The ability of PCA gene expression to detect T cell enrichment (3.8x) was higher than that of visual + gene fusion (STAIG 2.2x), indicating that T cell signals are mainly driven by gene expression rather than tissue morphology.
  • The strategy at the beginning of the Introduction: Directly presenting the most important numerical findings (ARI from 0.26 to 0.55) was more persuasive than providing a general background. Having one Results section title for each paragraph improved the consistency of the paper structure.
