Problems and Solutions#

Key Problems#

1. SpaceMouse collected experiments couldn’t be enhanced by MimicGen, while demonstrations generated by MimicGen worked.#

Solution: It was found that MimicGen requires complete datagen_info metadata (eef_pose, object_poses, target_pose, gripper_action, subtask_term_signals) and clear initial state semantics. SpaceMouse experiments usually start from incorrect states, lacking the required metadata. Two different demonstration processes were identified: recovery demonstrations (simplified format) and successful demonstrations (compatible format with MimicGen).

Key Insight: Format compatibility isn’t just about file structure—it also includes semantic assumptions (clear vs. incorrect states) and metadata completeness. The enhancement process for recovery demonstrations differs from the standard MimicGen source demonstrations.

2. Confusion between the term “training source demonstration” in different process contexts#

Solution: It was clarified that in the context of recovery, “training source demonstration” refers to “training recovery demonstration” (RecoveryDemo + AugmentedRecovery), which is different from MimicGen’s success_demos.hdf5. A clear document mapping was created: for manually collected RecoveryDemos, use outputs/recovery/demos/; for MimicGen-style enhanced demonstrations, use outputs/recovery/augmented/.

Key Insight: Technical terms have specific meanings within specific projects. In multi-process projects, it’s important to have clear context to avoid mapping errors. Prepared_source/*.hdf5 files are dependencies for enhancements, not the training source demonstration itself.

General Problems#

3. The document tone wasn’t suitable for the target audience (team-guided)#

Solution: The training_source_demo_dual_path.md document was rewritten twice. The first version had a “fixative” tone because it was created while debugging conceptual misunderstandings. The second version was rewritten to explain the process to team members, including clear flowcharts, file paths, and current status snapshots.

Key Insight: Documents generated during problem-solving often have a debugging narrative style. It’s important to clearly inform the audience that technical references and team guidance require different levels of detail.

Lessons Learned#

Main Lessons#

  • MimicGen’s source demonstrations require complete datagen_info metadata (eef_pose, object_poses, target_pose, gripper_action, subtask_term_signals) and semantic assumptions (clean initial state, complete task trajectory, valid subtask transitions). Recovery demonstrations from incorrect states require separate enhancement processes.
  • Format compatibility includes three levels: file structure (NPZ vs. HDF5), metadata completeness (datagen_info fields), and semantic assumptions (starting state, task coverage). A file may pass structural checks but may not meet semantic requirements.
  • In multi-pipeline projects, technical terms require explicit project-specific definitions. “Source demonstration” can mean different things in the recovery pipeline (RecoveryDemo) versus success_demos.hdf5 in the standard MimicGen pipeline. Documents should establish clear namespaces.
  • The LiPM paper demonstrated physical information-based training for battery tasks: MMAE for cross-modal coupling, CIR for Coulomb integration, dual-scale encoder for irregular sampling. Key insight: When physical constraints are correctly encoded, domain-specific baseline models outperform general time series models.

Practical Experience#

  • Documents generated during problem-solving inherit a debugging narrative style. For team onboarding documents, it’s necessary to clearly describe the current design rather than just finding its path. Audience awareness must be externally injected, rather than emerging spontaneously.
